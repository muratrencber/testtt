1. Introduction

1.1. Scope

CarShare360 is a platform enabling car owners to share their vehicles conveniently. Our service focuses on geolocation-based vehicle sharing via a user-friendly app. Car owners can easily list their cars for short-term rentals, fostering a collaborative and sustainable transportation solution. Importantly, CarShare360 excludes payment services, ensuring a straightforward and secure vehicle-sharing experience.

1.2. Deliverables

  1. Backend Development
  2. Serverless Design & Development
  3. Product Development & Admin Panel
  4. UI Design & Implementation
  5. Mobile Development

1.4. Non-Functional Issues

1.4.1. Performance

High performance is a critical aspect of CarShare360, ensuring a swift and responsive experience for both car owners and renters. It influences the speed at which users can locate available cars, make bookings, and update their profiles. Eﬀicient serverless architecture, database optimization, and load testing will be employed to guarantee that our app can handle a significant number of simultaneous users without any delays. A responsive and speedy platform enhances user satisfaction and encourages them to engage with the service more frequently.

1.4.2. Usability

Usability is pivotal in making CarShare360 an accessible and user-friendly platform, tailored for Android and iOS. The design and navigation must be intuitive, simplifying tasks for car owners when listing their vehicles and aiding renters in locating and booking cars effortlessly. A user-friendly interface will be a key driver of user adoption and retention.

1.4.3. Cross-Platform Compatibility

While CarShare360 is designed for mobile devices on Android and iOS, cross-platform compatibility remains a priority. It means that the app will provide a consistent and efficient experience across different smartphones and tablets. By optimizing for different mobile operating systems, including iOS and Android, we ensure that all users enjoy a seamless and user-friendly experience, leading to increased engagement and broader adoption.

4. Schedule

4.1. Overview

The schedule for the CarShare360 project is designed to ensure a systematic and efficient approach to achieving project milestones. The timeline has been structured to maximize resource allocation and provide sufficient time for development, testing, and deployment phases.

4.2. Major Milestones

The project is divided into several major milestones:

  1. Project Kick-off: Project initiation and team assignments.
  2. Requirement Analysis Completion: All system requirements are gathered and documented.
  3. Design Phase Completion: UI/UX designs and system architecture are finalized.
  4. Development Phase 1 Completion: Core functionalities for user authentication and car listings are implemented.
  5. Testing Phase 1 Completion: Initial testing round for core functionalities is completed.
  6. Development Phase 2 Completion: Additional features like geolocation and rating systems are implemented.
  7. Testing Phase 2 Completion: Comprehensive testing including stress and usability tests.
  8. Beta Release: The application is released to a limited user base for beta testing.
  9. Final Release: The application is launched publicly on all platforms.
  10. Post-Launch Review: The team evaluates project outcomes and identifies learning points.

4.4. PERT and Gantt Charts

To visualize the project timeline and dependencies, a PERT chart and a Gantt chart have been developed. These charts detail the sequence of project activities, their start and end dates, and the interdependencies between tasks.

4.5. Critical Path

The critical path for the project has been identified to ensure that the project is completed on time. The critical tasks are those that, if delayed, would delay the entire project. The critical path analysis will be used to monitor project progress and prioritize task completion.

4.6. Monitoring and Adjustments

The project schedule will be monitored closely, with regular status meetings to ensure that all tasks are on track. Adjustments to the timeline will be made as necessary in response to any deviations from the planned schedule or unexpected project risks.

4.7. Conclusion

The project schedule is built to be robust yet flexible, allowing for adjustments as needed while ensuring that the project moves forward towards successful completion within the designated timeframe.

5. Risks

5.1. Risk Impact and Probability Assessment

Effective risk management is crucial for the timely and successful delivery of the CarShare360 project. We have identified potential risks, assessed their probability of occurrence, and evaluated their potential impact on the project.

Risk Probability Impact Priority
Technical complexities in backend integration High High Critical
Unexpected delays in development milestones Medium High High
Changes in stakeholder requirements Medium Medium Medium
Non-compliance with data protection regulations Low Severe High
Difficulty in third-party service integration Medium Medium Medium

5.2. Risk Mitigation Strategies

Mitigation strategies have been developed for each identified risk, aiming to reduce both the likelihood of the risk event and its potential impact on the project.

Risk Mitigation Strategy
Technical complexities in backend integration Conduct a technical feasibility study; perform prototyping; engage in early architectural reviews; ensure team skill enhancement through training.
Unexpected delays in development milestones Implement agile methodology for iterative development; enforce project management best practices; perform regular progress tracking.
Changes in stakeholder requirements Regularly engage with stakeholders to clarify and prioritize requirements; use change management processes to handle requests systematically.
Non-compliance with data protection regulations Engage a compliance specialist; conduct regular audits; integrate privacy by design principles in the development process.
Difficulty in third-party service integration Choose reputable service providers with well-documented APIs; create a proof of concept; maintain open communication channels with service providers.

5.3. Monitoring and Review

Risks will be monitored on a bi-weekly basis during team meetings, and the Risk Register will be updated accordingly. The project manager is responsible for overseeing this process and ensuring that mitigation strategies are executed as planned. This proactive approach ensures that we can promptly respond to risks as they evolve throughout the project lifecycle.
